Why We Came Into Being?

The lockdown is getting to your nerves, right? Everyone is probably lying on their bed on the verge of going insane. Right? So, we are pretty much on the same page. Now in this situation, reduce your house to a one room apartment of 8 ft x 10 ft dimensions. You must be inhumane if your heart doesn’t start pounding at the thought. At least mine does. Now remove the luxuries from the room and add 4 more members to your family (you must be questioning your existence by now. Great! Keep up).
Stuff seems bad for you anyways, when you suddenly hear that your parents have lost their jobs, and no, they can’t file law suits against the employer because guess what? There was no legal contract for employment in the first place! Now pretend that you’re the great titan Atlas and you carry your apartment on your shoulder and deposit it in a cramped up, foul smelling, dirty locality we squinch our noses at, also called the ‘slums’. So, the luxury of flushing your toilets after having a good dump and holding showers aren't there.
And then, you start falling prey to your physiological needs, but there are really no provisions to maintain good hygiene during these times. Distress flooding in yet? Now, the lockdown is no more about exotic pastas or delicious cakes or dalgona coffee, in fact, you can barely get two square meals a day. Using the minimum amount of common sense left in the people of this age, we can very well figure out that if there’s no money for food, there is certainly no money for smart gadgets. Therefore, no internet, no education and ultimately, a year lost.
Oh! Did I forget to mention that the chances of spreading of Covid-19 are most in localities like the one you’re imagining to live in? Please note that too. Aren’t cries of frustration popping up in your mind? This is exactly how innumerable people have been spending their days for the last 5 months. They have long given up the want of education, proper clothing, let alone a decent lifestyle.
All they think day and night is about how to keep their bodies and souls together, a thought which with each passing day seems to turn more and more vague. They only want food enough to keep them breathing and the bare minimum facilities to not be rendered lifeless by the vicious all-consuming disease that is spreading mercilessly. Lucky you are if you don’t have to face these issues. With all luxuries available to us, this time seems to be unbearable.
I need not ask you think about helping them because this thought is the first humane one to come to us. We need to share our privileges with them. We at Caritas intend to do exactly that. Plans have been made to provide food and basic sanitation facilities, both of which shall be well sanitized and free from the contact of any dangerous virus or bacteria. Not only providing the less-privileged with basic amenities, we also try our best to generate awareness of this massive and unfair social difference amongst the urban populations. These measures, we are certain are the needs of the hour and not to be heavily focused on.

Ritika Shah
