What Non-Government Organisations Are All About?

One of the biggest and the most inspiring things a person can do is helping others out. There are people in the world who are extremely privileged and get more than they require or ask for. There are also people in the world who did not get so lucky and got less than they actually require. One of the responsibilities of the privileged group is to help out the underprivileged ones.

The main step is initiative. Once one person takes the initiative, it inspires other people to do the same. That is basically what an NGO does.

A Non- Governmental Organisation (NGO) is a non-profit organisation founded by the citizens whose main aim is providing educational, social and financial help to people who are unable to provide for themselves specially during times of crisis.
The International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement is the world’s largest group of humanitarian NGO’s.

A non-profit organization is mission-driven, which requires the management and board to set objectives aimed at achieving the organization’s stated mission.

Working for or with a non-profit organisation goes hand in hand with doing charity work. Charity basically means to dedicate your time to helping people in need voluntarily, that is, without anyone forcing us to do so.

The word charity originated in late Old English to mean a "Christian love of one's fellows.” Over time, the meaning of charity has shifted from one of "Christian love" to that of "providing for those in need; generosity and giving," a transition which began with the Old French word charité.

Charitable giving is the act of giving money or goods to the underprivileged either directly or through charitable trusts or NGOs. Charity includes not only the giving of money or goods but also includes actions like educating orphans, visiting the imprisoned among other things. For example, the "Make a wish foundation" in the USA, grants wish to the children between the age of 2 and 17, having a life-threatening disease.

Helping someone out in need does not only bring a smile on their faces, but also, gives us a feeling on inner satisfaction and peace. People even do charity work for selfish reasons like increasing their goodwill or their reputation, but nonetheless, even then someone is being helped.

It is always an individual's choice if they want to help others out but it is always a good idea to put a smile on someone's face and such acts should be encouraged.
